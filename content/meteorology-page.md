# Aviation Meteorology (DGCA CPL/IR) — Notes

> Base aligned to DGCA CAR Section 7, Series B, Part IV (Appendix B: Aviation Meteorology)

## 1) Atmosphere Basics
- **Composition:** Nitrogen ~78%, Oxygen ~21%, others ~1%
- **Layers:** Troposphere (weather zone), Tropopause, Stratosphere
- **ISA:** Sea-level temp 15°C, pressure 1013.25 hPa
- **Lapse rate:** Avg ~2°C per 1000 ft in troposphere

## 2) Pressure & Wind
- Wind flows from **high pressure to low pressure** (modified by Coriolis + friction)
- **Coriolis:** Right deflection in NH, left in SH
- **Gradient wind:** Parallel to isobars (aloft)
- **Surface wind:** Crosses isobars toward low pressure due to friction

## 3) Stability & Cloud Formation
- **Stable air:** Smooth, layered cloud (Stratus), poor vertical motion
- **Unstable air:** Turbulence, cumulus/CB, showers/thunderstorms
- **Cloud formation needs:** Moisture + cooling to dew point + lifting mechanism

## 4) Fog, Mist, Haze
- **Radiation fog:** Clear night, light wind, land cooling
- **Advection fog:** Warm moist air over colder surface
- **Upslope fog:** Moist air forced uphill
- **Runway hazard:** Rapid visibility reduction, illusions on approach

## 5) Fronts & Air Masses
- **Cold front:** Steep slope, showery rain, CB risk, gusty winds
- **Warm front:** Layered cloud, steady precipitation, poor visibility
- **Occluded front:** Mature cyclone feature, mixed weather

## 6) Thunderstorms (CB)
- Life cycle: **Cumulus → Mature → Dissipating**
- Hazards: Severe turbulence, lightning, hail, wind shear, microburst, icing
- Avoidance: Strategic deviation; do not penetrate active CB cells

## 7) Icing
- **Types:** Clear, rime, mixed
- **Most dangerous zone:** Around 0°C to -20°C with visible moisture
- Effects: Weight↑, drag↑, lift↓, stall speed↑

## 8) Turbulence & Wind Shear
- **Mechanical turbulence:** Near terrain/obstacles
- **CAT:** Clear-air turbulence near jet streams
- **Wind shear/microburst:** Major takeoff/landing threat

## 9) Altimetry & Temperature Errors
- **QNH:** Elevation above MSL on ground
- **QFE:** Zero at airfield elevation
- **QNE:** 1013.25 hPa (flight levels)
- Cold temperature error: True altitude lower than indicated

## 10) Jet Streams & Monsoon Context (India)
- Jet streams influence routing, turbulence, and fuel planning
- Monsoon patterns impact visibility, convection, and operational alternates

## 11) Aviation Weather Reports
- **METAR/SPECI:** Current observed weather
- **TAF:** Aerodrome forecast
- **SIGMET:** Significant en-route weather hazards
- **Trend interpretation** is exam-critical + operationally essential

## 12) Exam Strategy (DGCA)
- Master definitions + interpretation + scenario-based application
- Practice METAR/TAF decoding daily
- Build quick sheets for: fronts, icing, fog, pressure/wind laws
